# Kashmir Shaivism: A Brief Overview

The study of Kashmir Shaivism has been profoundly shaped by the work of [@sanderson2009], whose comprehensive survey of the Śaiva traditions in the early medieval period remains the standard reference.

## Philosophical Foundations

The doctrine of vibration (*spanda*) was systematically analysed by [@dyczkowski1987], building on the foundational *Īśvarapratyabhijñākārikā* edited and translated by [@torella1994]. For a broader introduction to the Hindu philosophical context, see [@flood1996].

## The Recognition School

Utpaladeva's recognition philosophy (*pratyabhijñā*) has been the subject of renewed scholarly attention. [@ratié2011] examines the epistemological arguments for consciousness as the ultimate ground of reality. [@nemec2011] explores Somānanda's *Śivadṛṣṭi* and its engagement with rival Tantric systems.

## Abhinavagupta's Synthesis

Abhinavagupta's philosophy of revelation is discussed by [@hanneder2006]. For detailed exegesis of his *Tantrasāra*, see [@sanderson2005a].

## Śaivism and the State

The relationship between Śaiva institutions and royal power is examined in [@sanderson2005b], while the broader exegetical traditions of Kashmiri Śaivism are surveyed in [@sanderson2007].
